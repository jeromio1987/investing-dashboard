🖥️ Emerging Sector: CPU Shortage / Semiconductor Bottleneck
The structural shift: AI inference at scale requires CPUs as much as GPUs. The first wave was GPU-dominated (NVDA). The second wave — running trained models for billions of daily queries — demands high-core-count server CPUs, high-bandwidth memory, and custom silicon. AMD's EPYC is taking share from Intel in AI inference racks. The bottleneck is now shifting to: networking chips (Broadcom, Marvell), advanced packaging (AMAT, LRCX), and test equipment.

🧠 SMID Behavioral Rules
What Kills SMID Returns
❌
Oversizing speculative names — 1 position at 5% that goes to zero wipes out 5 successful 1% positions that doubled. The math is brutal.
❌
Adding to clinical losers — "averaging down" into a biotech after −30% pre-data almost always ends in −70% total. The market is pricing information you don't have.
❌
Holding through a cash raise — If a SMID name you own announces an equity offering at a discount, sell the day of the announcement. Don't wait.
❌
Buying the spike on news you missed — Chasing POWL after it's already up 20% on earnings eliminates your risk-reward. Better to miss it and wait for the next quarter.
What Builds SMID Returns
✓
Patience before the catalyst — The edge is in being early, not being right in real-time. Build the position quietly 4–8 weeks before the catalyst window.
✓
Selling into strength — On a big beat or positive data: sell 30–50% into the move. Protect the gain. Let the rest run with a raised stop.
✓
The cross-read basket — Own the second mover at half size of the primary. BEAM + NTLA. ARVN + KYMR. NXE + UUUU. The basket multiplies one catalyst's value.
✓
Reading primary sources — With 3–8 analysts, your own 10-K read and earnings call transcript analysis can develop a genuinely differentiated view. This is the actual edge.
